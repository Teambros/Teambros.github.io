Healthy Food Choices for Optimal Health

How does the food you eat affect different aspects of your health?

Posted by
Jeff Lim
Related Url
http://www.unlitips.com
Share blog

You have had it said over and over that you are what you eat. Most of the times, we associate food with calories, fat, sugar, and nutrition. An interesting way to look at food is that it provides the materials and information the body needs for proper function. Without the right information, bodily processes will be affected, resulting in declined health.

You need to eat food in the right proportions to ensure that your body receives the right information. Too much food or any food that will give your body the wrong information results in problems like undernourishment, obesity, and diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and arthritis.

Metabolic processes

Without proper nutrition, body cells are unable to perform necessary functions, slowing down or even stopping metabolic processes. You, therefore, need to focus on eating healthy food for proper bodily function, and disease prevention.

Disease prevention

Healthy foods prevent you from developing chronic diseases like cancer. 60% of cancer cases are related to diet choices. Plant sources have been found to keep cancer at bay when compared to their animal sources. High sugar and fat foods result in obesity. There are around 100 million Americans who are obese. Obesity increases the risk of hypertension, Alzheimer’s disease, stroke, sleep apnea, heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and osteoarthritis.

Healthy foods boost the immune system, fortifying the body against communicable diseases as well.

Physical health

The food you eat determines things like your strength, coordination, performance levels, agility, and speed. it determines how you work, study, exercise and carries out your family duties. Without a proper diet, even the simplest of movements can be extremely strenuous. A proper diet ensures that you remain energetic throughout the day, and you are able to carry out your activities with ease.

Go for whole grains, vegetables, and fruits instead of carbs awash with sugar. The latter results in spikes and crashes in energy. Ensure that you also go for unsaturated fats in fish, nuts and vegetable oils instead of foods fried in saturated fats and trans fats which increase the risk for diseases that impair your physical wellbeing.

Cardiovascular health

A diet that consists of poultry, fish, nuts and whole grains will reduce the risk of developing hypertension. This should be combined with less salt, red meat, sweets, sodium, sugary drinks, and fats. Many processed foods tend to contain high levels of sodium. A healthy diet should be combined with exercise to ensure optimal blood flow and that the heart is pumping.

Keeping deficiencies at bay

Intake of a healthy diet ensures that your body accesses adequate nutrients and vitamins. These are vital for energy. development, and growth. inadequate vitamins result in deficiencies resulting in dizziness, weight loss, fatigue and even shortness of breath. A deficiency in Vitamin C, 812, and folate, for instance, causes anemia. if you have a vitamin deficiency, visit your doctor, so that you can determine the particular vitamin whose level is inadequate in your body. You may need to take multivitamins or other supplements.

Mental health

Food powers your brain. A healthy diet, with fewer calories, will improve learning and memory, as well as mental conditions like schizophrenia, Parkinson’s disease, and Alzheimer’s disease. A healthy diet for optimal mental health includes fruits, vegetables, fish (for example salmon, which high in Omega 3 fatty acids)

How do you ensure that your body is accessing the right foods?

The simple answer to the above question is ensuring that you go for healthy foods. However, did you know that even how you prepare food affects the nutrients it contains? Let’s look at some healthy food choices for optimal health, and other aspects of healthy foods:

1. Leafy greens - kale, broccoli rabe, Swiss chard, mustard greens, book soy, and spinach.

2. Olive oil - contains antioxidants (polyphenols) which prevent cancer from warping and causing diseases. In a study, people that consumed olive oil more, especially with vegetables reduced the risk of cancer by 12%.

3. Whole grains - for instance, brown rice contains a lot of fiber which keeps you feeling fuller.

4. Flaxeeds - these are rich in fiber. They also contain antioxidants (lignin’s) that prevent cancer. The seeds and the oil contain Omega-3 fatty acids.

5. Oatmeal - this helps to get rid of cholesterol. The long cooking kind is preferred.

6. Green tea - This tea is rich in polyphenols (antioxidants). It also boosts your metabolism rates.

7. Minerals - these include zinc, magnesium, calcium, and iron. A good source of calcium includes low-fat milk, fortified soy milk, and yogurt. Good sources of zinc are oysters, poultry, nuts, seafood (crab and lobsters), dairy products, and fortified breakfast cereals. Good magnesium sources are nuts, green leafy vegetables, beans, and whole grains. Good iron sources include liver, leafy green vegetables, soybeans, tofu, and quinoa.

Food portions

Eating smaller portions is a good way to control your calorie intake. Fruits and vegetables should be part of your main meals. They should, in fact, fill half of your plate.

Hydration

Your body requires a lot of water for the proper functioning of various body systems. Since water has no calories, it helps to maintain a healthy body weight. The best way to avoid sugary drinks is to keep a water bottle near your workspace throughout the day. This way, when you want to drink something, you will reach for the water.
