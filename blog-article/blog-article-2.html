Tips on How to Plan a Healthy Diet

The impact of healthy eating on mental and emotional health

Posted by
Jeff Lim
Related Url
https://www.unlitips.com
Share blog

Food does have an effect on the mood of a person. Some mental problems such as schizophrenia, ADHD, and Alzheimer disease can be as a result of consuming unhealthy diet. Some people have even been compelled to commit suicide just because of poor eating habits. On the other hand, eating foods that have less sugar or carbohydrates can help improve your mood. Fruits and vegetables are also important.

There are no rules when it comes to healthy eating. You don‘t have to forget about that dish that you really love. it’s about maintaining a healthy dietary pattern. Here are some tips that can help you plan and stick to a healthy diet.

1. Watch on your salt intake

Many of us assume that the more salt we put on the food the tastier it would become. However, too much salt is actually harmful to the body. Intake of too much sodium can increase risks of a heart attack, kidney disease, stroke, erectile dysfunction and memory loss. It can also cause high blood pressure. Instead of sodium, you can use spices and herbs such as garlic, black pepper, or curry powder to improve the flavor.

2. Consume healthy fats

Not all fats are bad. There are some that are healthy and there is nothing wrong with enjoying meals that have healthy fats. These ‘good’ fats can improve both physical and mental health. An example is foods that are rich in omega-3 fats. I know you may be wondering what difference there is between “good’ and ‘bad’ fats. Healthy fats include the monounsaturated fats and the polyunsaturated fats. Examples 0 foods that have monounsaturated fats include avocados, pumpkin seeds, sesame seeds, almonds, etc. polyunsaturated fats can be found in fatty fish, walnuts, and flaxseed. On the other hand, “bad’ fats are the trans fats that can be found in margarine, processed foods, fried foods, snacks etc.

3. Consider Proteins in your diet

Consuming foods that are high in proteins can help you be energetic. You can add high-quality protein to your diet by taking plenty of chicken, fish, soy, beans, seeds, and nuts.

4. Add Calcium

Our bones and teeth require calcium in order for them to stay strong as the years go by. The body also uses calcium to coordinate the nervous system. You can get calcium from foods beans, greens and vegetables, and from dairy products. Ever wondered why your teeth feel so weak after drinking soda? Too much intake of sugary drinks alcohol or caffeine can deplete your calcium stores in the body. It’s not just the teeth that are affected, but the bones too.

5. Ensure that you consume whole grains and healthy carbs

Fiber is essential to the body since it helps with the digestion process. Whole grains and healthy carbohydrates also provide energy to the body. Antioxidants that are found in whole grains can help protect your body from serious heart conditions and diabetes. There is a difference between healthy carbs and unhealthy carbs. Examples of ‘good carbs’ include vegetables, fruits, beans, and whole grains. ‘Bad carbs’ are unhealthy since they do not have fiber and nutrients. Examples include white rice, white flour, and refined sugar.

6. Eat a lot of fruits and vegetables

This may sound like a cliche but eating fruits and vegetables is really important. They have high nutrients value and have low calories. They are rich in fiber, antioxidants, minerals and vitamins. The recommended amount that you should consume in a day is five servings. Replace your processed snack with colorful fruits and vegetables.

7. Reduce intake of sugar

Too much of anything is bad, and the same applies to sugar. If you are trying to lose some weight, the last thing you need is too much sugar. Too much sugar has also been linked to diabetes. Other than candy and desserts, sugar can also be found in cereals, bread, margarine, canned soups, fast food, and so much more.
